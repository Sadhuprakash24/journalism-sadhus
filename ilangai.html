Captian Prabakaran
1.Velupillai Prabhakaran led the Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam (LTTE) during the Sri Lankan Civil War. 2.His aim was to establish an independent state for Tamil people in northern Sri Lanka. 3.Under his leadership, the LTTE became known as one of the most lethal guerrilla groups globally. 4.Prabhakaran was born in Valvettiturai in 1954 and faced discrimination against Tamils in post-independence Sri Lanka. 5.He formed the Tamil New Tigers (TNT) in 1972, which later evolved into the LTTE. 6.Prabhakaran initiated suicide bombings and enforced strict rules within the LTTE. 7.The LTTE engaged in conflict with the Sri Lankan government, possessing their own navy and air force. 8.India intervened in 1987 with peacekeeping forces but faced resistance from Prabhakaran due to a lack of Tamil autonomy. 9.Prabhakaran's ruthless tactics extended to the assassination of Indian Prime Minister Rajiv Gandhi in 1991. 10.Despite attempts at ceasefire facilitated by Norway in 2001, conflict resumed, leading to the eventual defeat of the LTTE. 11.Prabhakaran and his son were killed in 2009, marking the end of the LTTE's insurgency.
